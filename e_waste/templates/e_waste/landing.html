How to Dispose of E-Waste, and Why?


Component in E-waste contain toxic and hazardous material such as mercury, lead, cadmium, arsenic, bromine, beryllium will permeate into the earth and subsequently water sources as well as threaten the aquatic and human life if E-waste is not disposed in environmentally sound manner.


Disposal Procedure


1. Identify and separate e-waste from regular trash


2. Schedule a collection from your house through our system


3. Once the pickup is scheduled, a driver will be sent to collect the e-waste

What is E-Waste?


E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices like phones, laptops, and appliances. They can also refer to broken, non-working or obsolete TVs, PCs, air conditioners, washing machines and refrigerators.
